Discovering music

If you want to know how I find music, there are two main sites I use to discover music:
  • https://www.albumoftheyear.org/
  • https://rateyourmusic.com/
Both of those websites have lists for the user-voted best albums of the year, critic-voted best albums, recent releases, and a lot more. They are both great for getting a quick glance at new and popular music, and offer plenty of tools for discovering more niche music if that is what you want.
Other than that, I search through Spotify's recommendations, list to random public Spotify playlists, keep track of all the artists I like, and get word of mouth recommendations from friends.

Other links

  • https://open.spotify.com/user/kyleowens - My Spotify account.
  • https://github.com/albumsfromkyle/ - This website's GitHub repository (which is where it is hosted).
  • https://www.last.fm/ - Tool for tracking your Spotify stats and listening habits throughout the year if you don't like only waiting for Spotify wrapped.
  • https://exportify.net/ - Tool allowing you to export Spotify playlists as CSV files. This was used a lot when developing this website, and I use it to back-up my personal Spotify playlists.
  • https://www.music-map.com/ - Tool that lets your find/explore similar bands to whatever band you type in.
